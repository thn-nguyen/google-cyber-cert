## Vulnerability Assessment Report

### Overview
In this activity, I performed a vulnerability assessment on a small e-commerce company about the database server, that is available to the public. I identified potential security risks, evaluated their impact, and provide recommendations to mitigate risks.

---

### Scenario
You are a newly hired cybersecurity analyst for an e-commerce company. The company stores information on a remote database server, since many of the employees work remotely from locations all around the world. Employees of the company regularly query, or request, data from the server to find potential customers. The database has been open to the public since the company's launch three years ago. As a cybersecurity professional, you recognize that keeping the database server open to the public is a serious vulnerability.

You are tasked with completing a vulnerability assessment of the situation to communicate the potential risks to decision makers at the company. You must create a written report that explains how the vulnerable server is a risk to business operations and how it can be secured.

---

### Goals
- Evaluates risks to a publicly accessible database server used by an e-commerce company. 
- Identify threats that could impact data confidentiality, integrity, and availability (CIA triad).

---

### Approach
- This assessment uses a qualitative risk method based on NIST SP 800-30 Rev. 1. 
- Each threat evaluated likelihood and potential impact, on how it could affect to business operations and data security.

---

### Risk Assessment Summary
- Hacker: 9 / critical risk.
- Employee or User: 6 / high risk.
- Malware / ransomware: 9 / critical risk.

---

### What I Learned?
- Understand how vulnerability assessments are used to evaluate real-world security risks. 
- Apply the NIST SP 800-30 Rev. 1 framework to identify threats, estimate risk levels, and how technical issues could affect to business operations. 
- Practical security controls such as MFA, least privilege, and system monitoring.
